About

I am a Ph.D. candidate in Electrical Engineering at The Pennsylvania State University, advised by Prof. Weihua Guan. My research integrates engineering, biology, and medicine to build accessible point-of-care diagnostic devices. I focus on single-molecule nanopore sensing coupled with isothermal amplification (RPA, LAMP, CRISPR) and machine-learning-driven signal analysis for the rapid detection of infectious and neurodegenerative diseases.

My work spans the full development pipeline, from assay chemistry and microfluidic chip fabrication to embedded readout electronics and edge-computing integration. I have translated benchtop nucleic-acid assays into portable, sample-to-answer platforms targeting SARS-CoV-2, HIV, Mpox, and Alzheimer disease. I also helped establish and operationalize the Guan Lab at Indiana University, where I currently serve as a Visiting Scholar.

Focus: Democratizing accessible, affordable, and accurate diagnosis through single-molecule sensing, microfluidics, and intelligent data analytics.
