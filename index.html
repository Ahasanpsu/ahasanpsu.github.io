About

I am a device engineer and Ph.D. candidate in Electrical Engineering at The Pennsylvania State University, advised by Prof. Weihua Guan. My work centers on building portable biomedical systems that connect microfluidic sample handling, assay chemistry, optical or electronic readout, embedded hardware, and data analysis into practical point-of-care diagnostic platforms.

I design and fabricate microfluidic devices, integrate them with sensing modules and compact instrumentation, and translate benchtop molecular assays into portable workflows for real samples. My projects span nanopore sensing, RPA/LAMP/CRISPR assays, fluorescence detection, sample preparation, system packaging, Python-based analysis, and hardware-software integration for applications in SARS-CoV-2, HIV, Mpox, and Alzheimer disease.

Focus: Engineering integrated, reliable, and field-ready diagnostic devices through microfluidic design, system integration, single-molecule sensing, and intelligent data analytics.
