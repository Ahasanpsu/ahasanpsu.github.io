Teaching

Courses delivered as Assistant Professor and Lecturer at the Bangladesh University of Textiles (BUTEX) and BGMEA University of Fashion & Technology. Detailed course materials will be added.

Engineering Materials

Assistant Professor & Lecturer, IPE Department, BUTEX

An introduction to the structure, properties, processing, and performance of engineering materials, including metals, polymers, ceramics, and composites. The course connects atomic and microstructural concepts to mechanical behavior and material selection for engineering design. Course materials will be added.

Mechanical Engineering

Assistant Professor & Lecturer, IPE Department, BUTEX

Fundamental principles of mechanical engineering spanning statics, dynamics, thermodynamics, and the mechanics of machines, with emphasis on applying core theory to practical engineering problems. Course materials will be added.

Machine Design

Assistant Professor & Lecturer, IPE Department, BUTEX

Design and analysis of machine elements such as shafts, gears, bearings, fasteners, and springs, incorporating stress analysis, fatigue, and factor-of-safety considerations for reliable mechanical systems. Course materials will be added.

Machine Shop and Practice

Assistant Professor & Lecturer, IPE Department, BUTEX

A hands-on laboratory course covering conventional manufacturing operations, including turning, milling, drilling, and fitting, along with workshop safety, measurement, and fabrication practice. Course materials will be added.

Engineering Drawing

Assistant Professor & Lecturer, IPE Department, BUTEX

Principles of technical drawing and engineering graphics, covering orthographic and isometric projection, sectioning, dimensioning, and computer-aided drafting for the communication of engineering designs. Course materials will be added.

Automation and Control Engineering

Assistant Professor & Lecturer, IPE Department, BUTEX

An introduction to industrial automation and control systems, covering sensors and actuators, feedback control principles, programmable logic controllers, and the integration of control hardware in manufacturing processes. Course materials will be added.
